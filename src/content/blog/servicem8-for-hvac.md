## What HVAC Businesses Need From Job Management Software

HVAC and refrigeration work has specific operational requirements that most generic business software doesn't address:

**Refrigerant handling compliance.** Under the Ozone Protection and Synthetic Greenhouse Gas Management Act 1989 and the associated Regulations, refrigerant handling in Australia requires ARCtick licensing, mandatory logbooks, and record-keeping for refrigerant purchases, usage, recovery, and destruction. This documentation must be accurate and retrievable.

**Service contract management.** Commercial HVAC businesses typically manage ongoing maintenance contracts across multiple sites and systems. Scheduled services, reactive callouts, and warranty work all need to be tracked, dispatched, and invoiced consistently.

**Seasonal demand management.** The Australian climate creates brutal demand spikes — summer air conditioning season across the south and east, and year-round pressure in tropical regions. Managing a surge in volume without dropping service quality or compliance standards requires systems that scale.

**Multi-technician dispatch.** HVAC businesses with 3+ technicians need real-time visibility across the team to manage emergency callouts, avoid geographic inefficiency, and keep customers informed.

**Defect and warranty tracking.** HVAC installations carry warranty obligations and ongoing defect liability. Documenting installation conditions, commissioning data, and subsequent service history creates the paper trail that protects your business when warranty claims arise.

## ARCtick and Refrigerant Compliance in ServiceM8

ARCtick compliance is non-negotiable for Australian refrigeration and air conditioning technicians. The requirements include:

- Maintaining a refrigerant logbook (separate to job records, but cross-referenced)
- Recording refrigerant quantities purchased, used, recovered, and transferred
- Lodging annual refrigerant trading authorisation returns with the Department of Climate Change, Energy, the Environment and Water
- Keeping records for 5 years

ServiceM8's custom form builder lets you create refrigerant handling records that attach to individual jobs. When a technician tops up a system, recovers refrigerant, or replaces a component, they complete the refrigerant form on-site before the job is marked done. The record is stored with the job, timestamped and cross-referenceable.

This doesn't replace your formal ARCtick logbook — that's a separate regulatory requirement — but it creates a job-level record that matches the logbook entries, providing a second source of truth for audits.

## Commissioning and Installation Documentation

New HVAC installations require commissioning documentation — the record that the system was installed to manufacturer specifications, tested to operating parameters, and handed over to the client correctly.

This documentation matters for:
- Warranty validity (many manufacturers require commissioning records for warranty claims)
- Follow-up service (knowing the original installation conditions helps diagnose faults faster)
- Compliance with Australian standards (AS/NZS 3823, AS 1677 for refrigeration systems, and relevant BCA provisions)
- Defect liability (if something goes wrong within the defect liability period, the commissioning record shows what the system looked like at handover)

ServiceM8's digital form templates let you build commissioning checklists that mirror the requirements of your most common systems. Techs complete the commissioning form on-site before marking the job done, the completed form is stored with the job record, and a PDF can be emailed to the client as part of the project handover pack.
