## Pricing: Per-Job vs Per-User

This is the most important structural difference between the two platforms.

### ServiceM8 Pricing

ServiceM8 charges per-job, not per-user. The number of staff using the platform doesn't change your bill — only your job volume does.

| Plan | Monthly cost (AUD) | Jobs per month |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| Lite | ~$29 | 15 |
| Starter | ~$65 | 50 |
| Growing | ~$149 | 150 |
| Premium | ~$349 | Unlimited |

All staff — field technicians, office administrators, managers — can access the platform on all plans. As you hire, your software cost stays flat (until you hit the next job tier).

### TradiePad Pricing

TradiePad uses per-user pricing, which means your software cost scales linearly with your team size.

For businesses with growing teams, this difference compounds:

| Team size | ServiceM8 (Growing, 100 jobs/mo) | TradiePad (~$39/user) |
|---|---|---|
| 2 staff | $149 | ~$78 |
| 5 staff | $149 | ~$195 |
| 8 staff | $149 | ~$312 |
| 10 staff | $149 | ~$390 |

At smaller team sizes, TradiePad may be price-competitive or cheaper. At 4+ staff doing consistent job volume, ServiceM8's pricing model becomes significantly more economical.

## Compliance Documentation: A Major Differentiation

This is where the gap between ServiceM8 and TradiePad is most significant for Australian trade businesses.

### ServiceM8's Compliance Approach

ServiceM8 was built with compliance-heavy trades in mind — electrical, plumbing, solar, gas fitting — where documentation is regulatory requirement, not optional.

**Custom form builder.** Create any compliance form, checklist, or certificate template in ServiceM8. Certificates of Electrical Safety, CES forms for Victorian electricians, plumbing compliance certificates, solar installation checklists, SWMS templates — all can be built as digital forms that field staff complete on-site, before the job is marked done.

**Enforced completion.** Forms can be set as required. Field staff cannot mark a job complete without completing all required forms. This eliminates the common scenario of paperwork left incomplete because "we'll do it in the morning."

**Photo capture with metadata.** Photos taken through ServiceM8 are attached to the specific job record with timestamp and GPS coordinates. For CER audits, insurance claims, or compliance verification, these photos have documented provenance — tied to a specific job, date, and location, not sitting in a camera roll.

**Certificate generation.** Completed compliance form data can populate PDF certificates that are emailed to the client or stored with the job record. Solar installers commonly use this for CEC handover documentation. Electricians use it for CES generation.

This compliance infrastructure is what makes ServiceM8 genuinely valuable for Australian electricians, plumbers, solar installers, and other licensed trades.

### TradiePad's Compliance Approach

TradiePad offers file attachments and job notes, which allows compliance documentation to be stored against jobs. However, it doesn't have ServiceM8's custom form builder, enforced completion requirements, or GPS-tagged photo capture.

For trades where compliance documentation is a primary operational concern, this is a meaningful gap. You can store completed paper forms as scanned attachments in TradiePad, but you can't enforce that the forms are completed before a job is signed off.

For simpler trades where compliance documentation is less intensive, this distinction matters less.

## Scheduling and Dispatch

### ServiceM8 Dispatch Board

ServiceM8's visual dispatch board is one of its strongest features for businesses managing multiple field staff. You see all active jobs, all technicians, and their GPS positions simultaneously. Assigning, reassigning, and reordering jobs is visual and fast.

Automated client communication integrates directly with the dispatch workflow — when a job is assigned, the client automatically receives a booking confirmation. When the technician is on their way, they get an ETA notification. None of this requires manual action.

### TradiePad Scheduling

TradiePad offers calendar-based job scheduling that works well for straightforward workflow. For businesses with 2–3 field staff and predictable scheduling, it's adequate.

For businesses managing emergency callouts, multiple simultaneous crews, and dynamic rescheduling, ServiceM8's dispatch board provides more operational visibility.

ServiceM8's automation capabilities extend beyond simple reminders:

- **Booking confirmation** — automated SMS when a job is scheduled

## FAQ

### Is TradiePad Australian-built?

Yes, TradiePad is Australian-built, as is ServiceM8. Both platforms have been developed with the Australian trades market in mind, which gives them advantages over international software that doesn't account for Australian compliance requirements.

### Which platform has better reviews from Australian tradies?

ServiceM8 has a larger installed base in the Australian trades market and consistently strong reviews across multiple review platforms. TradiePad has positive reviews for simplicity and ease of use. For compliance-heavy trades, ServiceM8 reviews tend to specifically highlight the compliance documentation tools as a deciding factor.

### Can I migrate from TradiePad to ServiceM8?

Yes. ServiceM8's onboarding support can help with data migration from other platforms. Client records and job history can be imported. The compliance form templates will need to be rebuilt within ServiceM8's form builder, but this is typically done as part of setup.

### Does ServiceM8 work for sole traders?

Yes. The Lite plan (~$29/month, 15 jobs) is designed for sole traders and works well for lower-volume businesses. As you grow, you step up plans. The per-job pricing model means sole traders pay less than larger teams automatically.
