## Compliance Documentation

Both platforms have compliance documentation capabilities, but they approach it differently.

### ServiceM8

ServiceM8's compliance tooling is purpose-built for the way Australian licensed trades actually work:

**Custom form builder.** Any compliance form — CES templates for electricians, plumbing compliance certificates, AS/NZS 5033 solar installation checklists, SWMS for high-risk work, ARCtick refrigerant records — can be built as a digital form. Field staff complete it on-site, on their phone, before the job is marked done.

**Enforced completion.** Forms can be set as mandatory — no job close-out without every required field completed. This eliminates the most common compliance failure: paperwork that doesn't get done because no one was watching.

**GPS-tagged photos.** Photos taken through the ServiceM8 app attach to the specific job record with timestamp and GPS coordinates. For CER audits, insurance claims, or licensing reviews, photos have documented provenance.

This approach works well for the compliance-heavy trades that make up the majority of ServiceM8's user base — electricians, solar installers, plumbers, gas fitters, HVAC technicians.

### AroFlo

AroFlo has comprehensive compliance and safety management tools, including more sophisticated permit-to-work management, safety inspection scheduling, and integrated asset maintenance compliance. For larger operations managing complex compliance frameworks — preventive maintenance programs across large asset portfolios, contractor compliance management, enterprise safety management — AroFlo's compliance features are more powerful.

For a 5-person electrical business managing CES documentation and solar installation compliance, AroFlo's compliance depth is significantly more than required — and the setup to configure it correctly adds weeks to your implementation.

## Job Costing and Financial Reporting

### AroFlo's Advantage

AroFlo has more sophisticated financial reporting than ServiceM8. Detailed job costing with multiple cost centres, project budgeting, progress billing, comprehensive profitability reports by client, job type, division, and period — AroFlo's financial reporting is built for businesses that need to manage complex job economics.

For trade businesses with multiple business units, complex project revenue recognition, or significant commercial project work, AroFlo's financial reporting provides visibility that ServiceM8 doesn't match.

### ServiceM8's Approach

ServiceM8 provides functional job costing — time tracking, materials logging, and job cost summary — alongside Xero/MYOB integration that handles detailed accounting. For most small to mid-size trade businesses, this combination is sufficient. Detailed financial analysis lives in Xero or MYOB; ServiceM8 feeds it.

For businesses with very sophisticated financial management requirements at the job level, AroFlo's reporting may justify the cost premium.
