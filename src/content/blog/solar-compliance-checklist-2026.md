If you're installing solar panels in Australia, compliance isn't optional — it's the foundation of your business.

The Clean Energy Regulator (CER) has been tightening enforcement. In recent years, installer accreditation reviews have increased significantly, and the penalties for non-compliance are getting steeper. In 2023–24 alone, the CER completed over 800 installer audits and took action against dozens of businesses for documentation failures — not substandard physical work.

This checklist covers everything you need to have in order **before, during, and after** every solar installation job.

## Before the Job

### CER Accreditation
- ✅ Current CEC accreditation (check expiry date — accreditation lapses mid-year for many installers)
- ✅ Correct endorsement categories for the work being performed (solar PV, battery storage, or both)
- ✅ Professional indemnity insurance up to date and valid for the installation type
- ✅ Electrical licence valid in your state/territory (check for any state-specific endorsements required)
- ✅ Any subcontractors on site also hold valid accreditation for their scope of work

### Design Compliance
- ✅ System design compliant with **AS/NZS 5033** (Installation and safety requirements for PV arrays)
- ✅ Inverter listed on the CEC Approved Inverter List
- ✅ Panels listed on the CEC Approved Modules List
- ✅ String sizing calculations documented and signed off
- ✅ Shading analysis completed (particularly important for systems with string inverters)
- ✅ Roof structural assessment completed (if required by site conditions or system size)
- ✅ Maximum system size confirmed against DNSP connection approval

### Customer Documentation
- ✅ Written quote with full system specifications (brand, model, rated output for all components)
- ✅ Pre-installation checklist signed by customer (confirming they understand what's being installed)
- ✅ Grid connection application submitted to DNSP and approval received (or pending confirmation)
- ✅ STC assignment form prepared (to be signed at completion)
- ✅ Customer informed about metering requirements and any tariff changes

## During Installation

### Electrical Standards
- ✅ All wiring compliant with **AS/NZS 3000** (Wiring Rules)
- ✅ DC isolator installed and accessible, labelled correctly per **AS/NZS 5033**
- ✅ AC isolator installed at switchboard
- ✅ Earthing and bonding per manufacturer specifications and AS/NZS 3000
- ✅ Cable management — UV-rated conduit for all exposed DC cabling
- ✅ Minimum separation maintained between DC and AC wiring where parallel runs occur
- ✅ All cable terminations inspected and secured

### Safety Procedures
- ✅ Working at heights procedures followed per WHS regulations for your state/territory
- ✅ Electrical safety procedures documented and communicated to all on-site workers
- ✅ Site-specific risk assessment completed before work commences
- ✅ PPE requirements met (fall protection, electrical insulation, UV protection)
- ✅ Emergency procedures communicated — location of isolation points confirmed with all workers
- ✅ Safety observer present for roof work where required by state WHS regulations

### Documentation During Install
- ✅ Progress photos of key stages: roof layout pre-wire, roof layout post-wire, DC isolator installation, switchboard modifications, inverter mounting, inverter commissioning screen
- ✅ Serial numbers recorded for **all panels** and **inverters** (photograph the labels as backup)
- ✅ Battery serial number and firmware version recorded (if applicable)
- ✅ Any variations from the approved design documented and approved by supervising electrician
- ✅ Conductor test results recorded before energisation

## After Installation

### Testing & Commissioning
- ✅ Insulation resistance testing completed (values recorded)
- ✅ Earth continuity testing completed (values recorded)
- ✅ Polarity verification at array, combiner (if applicable), and inverter input
- ✅ Functional testing of all DC and AC isolators
- ✅ RCD/safety switch testing at switchboard
- ✅ System performance verification — output matches expected spec at commissioning conditions
- ✅ Inverter registered on monitoring platform (if provided) and customer access enabled

### Paperwork
- ✅ Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES) completed and lodged with the relevant state electrical safety authority
- ✅ CEC installation details submitted within required timeframe (typically within 10 business days)
- ✅ STC assignment form completed and signed by customer **on the day of installation**
- ✅ Grid connection confirmation received from DNSP (export limiting settings confirmed if required)
- ✅ Metering order lodged with electricity retailer (if new metering required)
- ✅ Customer handover pack provided (system manual, warranty documents, monitoring app guide, emergency isolation guide)

### STC (Small-scale Technology Certificate) Documentation
- ✅ System details on STC claim match installed equipment exactly (brand, model, serial numbers, rated output)
- ✅ Installation date recorded accurately — STCs must be claimed within 12 months of installation
- ✅ Installer accreditation number present on all documentation
- ✅ Customer declaration form signed (confirming receipt of handover pack)
- ✅ Photos stored as evidence (minimum: roof array, switchboard, isolators, inverter label with serial number)
- ✅ STC paperwork stored securely — minimum 5 years from installation date

## Battery Storage Jobs — Additional Requirements

If your job includes a battery energy storage system (BESS), you're working under a separate and more demanding standard. AS/NZS 5139:2019 has specific requirements that go significantly beyond the solar PV checklist:

### AS/NZS 5139 Specific Checks
- ✅ Battery installed in an approved location (garage, dedicated battery room, outdoor enclosure — not living spaces)
- ✅ Minimum clearances maintained from ignition sources, switchboards, and gas meters
- ✅ Ventilation requirements met for the specific battery chemistry (vented vs. sealed systems differ significantly)
- ✅ Emergency isolation switch installed and clearly labelled per AS/NZS 5139
- ✅ Hazard signage installed at all required locations (entry points, near the battery, near electrical panels)
- ✅ Battery management system (BMS) confirmed operational
- ✅ Customer briefed on emergency shutdown procedure and fire response instructions

## State-Specific Considerations

Compliance in Australia isn't fully uniform — each state and territory has its own electrical licensing requirements, Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES) lodgement processes, and in some cases specific rules around metering and grid connection:

- **NSW** — Fair Trading requires CES lodgement within 3 business days of inspection. The Inspector of Electrical Work must be notified for certain system sizes.
- **VIC** — Energy Safe Victoria has specific requirements around solar design documentation and export limiting. Always check current DNSP requirements.
- **QLD** — Electrical Safety Office requires licensed electrical contractors to complete and issue an ESREQ for new connections and major work.
- **SA** — Consumer and Business Services has specific requirements for Certificate of Compliance (COC) documentation.
- **WA** — EnergySafety WA requirements differ from eastern states; check current requirements for solar installations specifically.

When working across multiple states, always verify the current local requirements — they change, and interstate differences catch out even experienced installers.

## Common Audit Failure Points

After reviewing CER audit findings data and speaking with installers who've been through the process, the most frequent documentation failures are:

1. **STC forms with errors** — wrong serial numbers, incorrect installation date, missing customer signature
2. **Photos that can't be verified** — images in camera rolls without timestamps, or photos that can't be definitively linked to the specific job
3. **Missing compliance checklists** — jobs where no formal pre/during/post checklist was completed and stored
4. **Expired accreditation** — installers whose CEC accreditation lapsed and wasn't renewed before the install date
5. **Battery jobs missing AS/NZS 5139 documentation** — the additional battery requirements are often overlooked when photovoltaic compliance is otherwise solid

Paper checklists and photos buried in camera rolls won't cut it when the CER auditor calls. The key to audit readiness is systematic, job-by-job compliance documentation — not a last-minute scramble.

## Staying Current as Standards Change

Australian solar compliance standards are not static. The CEC updates its installation guidelines periodically. AS/NZS standards are reviewed on a rolling cycle. DNSP requirements for export limiting and metering continue to evolve as grid penetration of solar increases.

The most reliable way to stay current:
- Subscribe to CEC installer communications (free as part of your accreditation)
- Follow your state electrical safety regulator for local rule changes
- Monitor AEMC and AER publications for grid connection policy changes
- Use a job management platform that your installation network uses — when standards change, the platforms update their template forms, which prompts you to review your own checklists

We update this checklist as standards change. The biggest pending update to watch in 2026 is the ongoing review of export management frameworks across all states — particularly export limiting requirements that may affect system sizing calculations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What compliance standards apply to solar installations in Australia in 2026?**

The primary standards are AS/NZS 5033 (PV arrays), AS/NZS 3000 (Wiring Rules), and CEC installation guidelines. Battery storage adds AS/NZS 5139:2019. Installers must hold current CEC accreditation and valid state/territory electrical licences.

**What documentation does the CER require?**

The CER requires a completed STC assignment form signed by the customer, proof of CEC accreditation, Certificate of Electrical Safety (CES), installation photos at key milestones, serial numbers for all components, and documentation confirming installed equipment matches the approved design.

**How long do I need to keep solar installation records?**

Minimum 5 years from the date of installation. This includes STC documentation, checklists, photos, certificates, and customer sign-off documents. Cloud-based [digital job management platforms](/blog/digital-job-management-solar-installers) handle retention automatically.

**What are the most common reasons for failing a CER audit?**

Incomplete compliance checklists, STC forms with errors or missing customer signatures, photos that can't be tied to a specific job and date, missing AS/NZS 5139 documentation for battery jobs, and expired accreditation at the time of installation.

**Do I need a separate checklist for battery storage?**

Yes. [AS/NZS 5139](/blog/as-nzs-5139-battery-storage-compliance) has specific requirements around labelling, ventilation, emergency isolation, and hazard signage that go beyond the standard solar PV checklist. A combined solar + battery job requires both sets of compliance documentation.
