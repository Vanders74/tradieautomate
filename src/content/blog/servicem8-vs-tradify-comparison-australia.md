## Pricing: Closer Than You'd Think, But Not Equal

### ServiceM8

ServiceM8 starts at around **$29/month AUD** and scales with your team size and plan features. For most small solar or electrical businesses (1–10 technicians), you'll typically land in the $59–$129/month range.

Key pricing points:
- 14-day free trial — full feature access, no credit card required
- Plans are transparent and scale predictably
- No implementation fees or forced onboarding costs
- Xero and MYOB integration included in most plans

### Tradify

Tradify starts at around **$49/month AUD** for a single user and increases as you add staff. The pricing model is per-user, which can add up quickly for teams above 3–4 people.

Key pricing points:
- 14-day free trial available
- Per-user pricing means costs scale linearly with headcount
- Generally slightly more expensive than ServiceM8 at equivalent team sizes
- No major implementation costs — setup is genuinely quick

**Verdict:** ServiceM8 generally offers more value per dollar, particularly for teams where compliance documentation and automation features are important. Tradify is competitive for very small teams doing high-volume simple jobs.

## Ease of Use: Both Are Simple, But Different

Both ServiceM8 and Tradify are designed for tradies, not enterprise software consultants. Neither requires weeks of training. But they have different strengths.

### ServiceM8: Fast and Field-Ready

ServiceM8 is purpose-built for field use. The mobile app is genuinely excellent — a solar installer can open a job, run through a compliance checklist, take photos, capture a customer signature, and send an invoice without leaving the app.

The dispatch board is clean and visual. Scheduling jobs, assigning them to technicians, and tracking progress is fast and intuitive. For businesses where the owner or team lead is on-site daily, this mobile-first design is exactly right.

Most new users are operational within a day or two.

### Tradify: Extremely Simple, Almost Minimal

Tradify's strength is its simplicity. The workflow is lean: create a quote, convert it to a job, dispatch a technician, send an invoice. If that's 80% of your business, Tradify is very easy to use.

The trade-off is that Tradify's simplicity becomes a limitation as your compliance needs grow. There's no equivalent to ServiceM8's custom forms builder. Photo capture works, but it's basic. Documentation features are functional rather than comprehensive.

For a plumber doing straightforward maintenance and repair work, Tradify's simplicity is an asset. For a solar installer managing CER compliance documentation, it starts to show limits.

**Verdict:** Both are easy to use. ServiceM8 is slightly more capable in field workflows; Tradify is marginally simpler to get started.

For Australian solar installers and electricians, compliance is the whole game. CER audits, AS/NZS standards, STC documentation — this paperwork must be complete, accurate, and retrievable.

### ServiceM8

ServiceM8 is purpose-designed for this kind of compliance-heavy work. Custom forms mean you can build your entire CER compliance workflow directly into the job process — technicians can't close a job without completing the required documentation. Photos are automatically attached to the job record. Customer signatures are stored digitally.

### Tradify

Tradify supports file attachments and basic job notes, but it doesn't have the custom forms capability that makes ServiceM8 so useful for compliance-heavy trades. You can attach photos and documents, but there's no way to build enforced compliance checklists or structured form capture.

**Verdict:** ServiceM8 wins clearly on compliance documentation — it's not close.
