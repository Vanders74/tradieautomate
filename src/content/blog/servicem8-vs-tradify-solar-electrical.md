## The Compliance Reality for Solar and Electrical Businesses

Before comparing the platforms, it's worth being explicit about what compliance documentation actually means for solar installers and electricians:

**Solar installation compliance (every job):**
- AS/NZS 5033 installation checklist
- AS/NZS 5139 battery storage documentation (where batteries are included)
- CEC Small-scale Technology Certificate (STC) documentation
- Customer handover documentation with system specs and instructions
- Labelling compliance photos (array label, performance label, main switch label)
- Certificate of Electrical Safety (state-specific)

**Electrical compliance (every job):**
- Certificate of Electrical Safety or equivalent (varies by state)
- Test records for the work performed
- Compliance photos (switchboard, installation, meter)
- Network distributor notification (for relevant work types)

**For CER audits:**
The Clean Energy Regulator can request documentation for any installation within the 5-year retention period. When they do, you need to produce complete installation records — not "I think we took photos, let me check Jake's phone."

The platform you choose needs to make this documentation systematic, enforced, and retrievable.

## Compliance Documentation: The Critical Difference

### ServiceM8

ServiceM8's custom form builder is the platform's most important feature for solar and electrical businesses. Here's how it works:

**Build once, enforce forever.** You create a solar installation compliance form that includes every field required by AS/NZS 5033, STC documentation requirements, and your state's electrical certificate format. You set this form as required for all solar installation jobs. Every technician, on every job, completes every field — or the job stays open.

**GPS-tagged photos with provenance.** When a solar installer photographs the array label, the system performance label, or the inverter installation, that photo attaches to the specific job record with:
- Timestamp (date and time of capture)
- GPS coordinates (verifying the photo was taken at the installation address)
- Technician identity (who took the photo)

For a CER audit, these photos are the difference between "here are my compliant installation records" and "I'll need to go through my camera roll."

**Certificate generation.** Your CES or electrical compliance certificate can be a fillable template in ServiceM8 that auto-populates from job data — client name, property address, installation date, technician licence number, work performed. The completed PDF is stored with the job record and can be emailed to the client automatically.

**5-year retrievable records.** Every job record in ServiceM8 — forms, photos, invoices, client communications, notes — is retained and searchable. A CER audit request for documentation from 18 months ago is answered in minutes, not hours.

### Tradify

Tradify allows file attachments and job notes, which means you can store compliance documents against jobs. But there are three significant limitations for solar and electrical businesses:

1. **No enforced completion.** There's no mechanism in Tradify to prevent a job being marked complete without specific compliance documentation present. Compliance depends entirely on technician discipline.

2. **No GPS metadata on photos.** Photos attached to Tradify jobs don't carry GPS coordinates or automatically link to a specific job record with the same provenance as ServiceM8. This matters for compliance photo evidence.

3. **No custom form builder.** Tradify doesn't have a form builder that lets you create AS/NZS 5033 checklists, STC documentation forms, or CES templates as native, enforceable forms. Documentation has to be managed externally and attached.

For a solar or electrical business managing CER compliance risk, this gap is significant.

## Scheduling for Solar Installation Businesses

Solar installation scheduling has specific characteristics:

**Crew requirements.** Installations typically need two people — a CEC-accredited designer/installer and a support technician. Job assignment needs to account for crew configuration.

**Accreditation matching.** Different jobs may need different CEC accreditation categories. Dispatch needs to consider what each technician is accredited for.

**Weather dependency.** Rooftop installations are weather-dependent. When rain delays jobs, rapid rescheduling and crew redeployment are needed.

ServiceM8's visual dispatch board shows all jobs, all crews, their GPS positions, and their qualifications. Dynamic rescheduling is visual and fast. Tradify's calendar scheduling handles straightforward scheduling, but the real-time dispatch visibility isn't the same.
