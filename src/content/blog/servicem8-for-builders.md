## What Builders Need From Job Management Software

**Quote accurately and quickly.** Residential renovation quoting is complex — scope changes, selections, allowances, and variations are constant. Your software needs to support detailed quoting without becoming a full-time administrative task.

**Manage subcontractors.** As a builder, you're often the principal contractor coordinating trades — electricians, plumbers, tilers, plasterers. Tracking who's doing what, when, and at what cost is central to running a profitable build.

**Document compliance.** The National Construction Code (NCC), state building regulations, and council requirements create a compliance documentation obligation that follows every job. Inspections, certificates of occupancy, waterproofing compliance — these must be documented and retained.

**Invoice on schedule.** Progress payment schedules, deposit invoices, and final invoices need to go out on time and without errors. Late invoicing disrupts project cash flow in ways that compound across a build.

**Keep clients informed.** Residential clients are emotionally invested in their project. The builder who communicates consistently — progress updates, schedule changes, budget tracking — keeps clients confident. The one who doesn't fields constant worried phone calls.

### Subcontractor Management

In ServiceM8, your subcontractors can be added as staff members with limited access — they see their assigned jobs, can update job status, complete required forms, and upload photos. This gives you visibility into their progress without requiring them to have full system access.

Alternatively, use ServiceM8 to manage the booking and documentation side of subcontractor coordination — creating work orders for each trade, tracking their completion status, and maintaining the compliance documentation the subcontractor provides.

### Compliance Documentation

Building compliance documentation in Australia flows from:

- **National Construction Code (NCC/BCA)** — structural and performance requirements
- **State building regulations** — notification and inspection requirements
- **Council requirements** — development approval conditions
- **Australian Standards** — specific technical standards referenced by the NCC

ServiceM8's form builder lets you create checklists and inspection records that match your specific compliance workflow. Pre-slab inspection checklist, frame inspection checklist, wet area waterproofing compliance record, plumbing rough-in inspection — all can be built as digital forms that complete on-site with photo evidence attached.

For Victorian builders, the Victorian Building Authority (VBA) sets out mandatory inspection stages for Class 1 buildings. Having digital completion records for each inspection stage, with photos and timestamps, creates a defensible audit trail.

### Progress Invoicing

Construction projects run on progress payment schedules. ServiceM8 supports staged invoicing — creating separate invoices for deposit, slab stage, frame stage, lockup, fit-out, and practical completion — all linked to the same project job record.

Each invoice can be generated and sent when the relevant stage is reached, with the full job context maintained across the project. Integration with Xero or MYOB means your accountant has real-time visibility into the project revenue recognition.

## Melbourne and Victoria: Local Regulatory Context

For Victorian builders, the VBA administers the licensing and compliance framework under the Building Act 1993 and Building Regulations 2018. Key requirements include:

**Domestic Building Work:**
- Mandatory stage inspections for Class 1 buildings (footings, frame, wet areas, insulation, final)
- Compliance certificates from relevant trades (Certificate of Electrical Safety, plumbing compliance certificate)
- Owner-builder permits where applicable
- Domestic Building Insurance (DBI) for projects over $16,000

**HIA and MBA compliance:**
If you're a member of the Housing Industry Association or Master Builders Association of Victoria, you likely have compliance document templates. These can be digitised and loaded into ServiceM8 as form templates.

**Waterproofing compliance:**
AS 3740 waterproofing requirements are frequently a point of failure in residential compliance audits. Building ServiceM8 waterproofing completion records with mandatory photos into your bathroom and wet area workflows addresses this risk systematically.
