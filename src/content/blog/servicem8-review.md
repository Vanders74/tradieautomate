## ServiceM8 Features: What You Actually Get

### Job Management and Scheduling

The dispatch board is where you'll spend most of your time. It's a visual calendar with drag-and-drop job allocation — you see every job, every staff member, and move things around without a chain of phone calls.

What makes it useful in practice:
- **Live staff map** — see exactly where your crew are at any moment, GPS-tracked via the app
- **Real-time job status updates** — the office sees a job progressing as the tech works through it on-site
- **Automated job confirmations** — customers get an SMS confirmation when the job is booked, then a reminder 24 hours before the appointment
- **Reassignment on the fly** — if something changes on-site, you can reallocate from the office immediately

For solar businesses running multiple crews across residential and commercial work, this single feature saves hours per week in coordination calls.

### Quoting and Invoicing

ServiceM8's quoting and invoicing workflow is built for speed:
- Build quotes from a product and service library — no retyping line items every time
- Convert an accepted quote to a job in one click
- **Auto-invoice on job completion** — when a tech marks a job done, the invoice goes out automatically
- Customers pay via the invoice link — no chasing, no calls
- Automated payment reminders at set intervals if the invoice sits unpaid

The auto-invoicing is the biggest cash flow improvement most trade businesses get from the platform. The average ServiceM8 user compresses their time-to-invoice from days or weeks to same-day. For a solar business doing 15–25 jobs per month at $5,000–$15,000 per install, that's a significant amount of cash no longer locked in "work done, not yet billed."

Integrations with Xero and MYOB keep your accounts in sync automatically — no double entry.

### Compliance Forms and Photo Capture

This is where ServiceM8 becomes genuinely important for solar and electrical businesses in Australia.

**Custom form builder:** Every job can include forms and checklists that technicians must complete before the job can be marked done. For solar installers, this means:
- Pre-installation compliance checks happen on-site, on the device, before the tech drives away
- AS/NZS requirements are captured digitally with timestamps attached to the job record
- CEC documentation is permanently filed against the job — not in someone's email or a random folder

**Photo capture:** When a tech takes a compliance photo through the ServiceM8 app, it attaches directly to that job with a timestamp and GPS location. Not in a camera roll. Not in a WhatsApp group. Attached to the job, indefinitely, retrievable in seconds.

If the Clean Energy Regulator requests installation photos from 18 months ago, you pull them up immediately. That's the difference between a ten-minute export and a two-hour document scramble. **Digital signatures:** Customer sign-off is captured on a tablet at the time of installation. Stored against the job record. No paper, no scanning, no chasing signatures after the fact.

We have covered the compliance angle in depth in our guide to [ServiceM8 for solar installers](/blog/servicem8-for-solar-installers). Worth reading if compliance documentation is your primary driver. ### GPS Tracking and Staff Management

ServiceM8 tracks staff location via their phones when the app is active. From the office, you have a live map of where everyone is.

Practical applications:
- Know when staff arrive on-site and when they leave
- Identify when a job is running long and reassign capacity
- Accurate time-on-site records for labour billing disputes

For sole traders, this feature is less relevant. For businesses with two or more crews working across different suburbs, the visibility alone is worth the subscription.

### Client Communications

ServiceM8 handles customer comms automatically across the full job lifecycle:
- **Job confirmation SMS** sent when a booking is made
- **ETA notification** when the tech is on the way, with a live tracking link
- **Job completion notification** with invoice link attached
- **Payment receipts** sent automatically when payment is received

For most trade businesses, this eliminates a significant chunk of inbound enquiry calls — "what time is the tradie coming?" — and creates a professional experience that drives referrals and repeat business.

### Google Review Automation

ServiceM8's review automation is one of its most underrated features.

After a job is completed and invoiced, an automated follow-up message goes out asking the customer for a Google review. Simple, systematic, consistent — every time, for every customer.

Businesses using this system average 4.8+ stars on Google. Not because their work is categorically better than competitors, but because they ask every single customer rather than hoping the satisfied ones volunteer.

That review velocity compounds. More reviews drives higher local search visibility. Higher local visibility means more enquiries without paying for more ads.

## ServiceM8 Cons

- **iOS-first** — the iOS app is best-in-class; the Android version is solid but occasionally lags on updates
- **Not built for complex project accounting** — multi-stage commercial projects with detailed cost tracking are better served by simPRO
- **No built-in estimating for large tenders** — if you're pricing $500K commercial solar projects, you'll need a separate tool
- **Limited inventory and materials management** — stock tracking is basic compared to enterprise platforms
- **Web interface less comprehensive than the app** — most functionality lives in the mobile app; the web dashboard is useful for scheduling and reporting but is not the primary power interface
