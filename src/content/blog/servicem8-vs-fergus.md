## Compliance Documentation: ServiceM8's Core Strength

This is the area where ServiceM8 has the most meaningful advantage for Australian licensed trades.

### ServiceM8 Compliance Features

**Custom form builder.** Build any compliance form, checklist, or certificate template inside ServiceM8. Australian electricians build CES and CCEW templates. Plumbers build compliance certificate templates matched to their state regulator's requirements. Solar installers build AS/NZS 5033 and 5139 installation checklists. These templates enforce completion — field staff can't mark a job done without completing all required forms.

**GPS-tagged photo capture.** Every photo taken through the ServiceM8 app is attached to the specific job record with timestamp and GPS coordinates. For CER audits, insurance claims, or compliance reviews, photos have provenance — tied to a specific job, date, and location.

**Certificate PDF generation.** Completed compliance forms can generate client-facing PDF certificates that auto-populate from job data. This is used by electricians for compliance certificate generation, by solar installers for handover documentation, and by plumbers for compliance certificate generation.

This compliance infrastructure is central to ServiceM8's value proposition for Australian licensed trades. It's the reason electricians, plumbers, and solar installers consistently rate it as their preferred platform.

### Fergus Compliance Features

Fergus has form and document functionality, and it's been improving. But the compliance form depth, enforcement mechanisms, and GPS photo metadata in ServiceM8 are currently more mature.

For trade businesses where compliance documentation is a regulatory obligation — not just a record-keeping preference — this difference is material.

## Job Costing and Margin Tracking: Fergus's Core Strength

This is where Fergus genuinely leads. The platform was built with job-level profitability visibility as a primary design goal, and it shows.

### Fergus Job Costing

Fergus provides detailed, real-time job cost tracking:
- Labour hours logged by each technician, costed at your rate
- Materials logged against jobs with supplier cost and your charge-out price
- Subcontractor costs allocated per job
- Real-time margin percentage calculated as the job progresses
- Comparison of estimated versus actual cost at job completion

The job profitability report in Fergus lets owners see exactly which jobs, job types, clients, and technicians are generating the best margins — and which ones are underperforming.

For businesses where the owner has a strong analytical focus on profitability by job or client segment, Fergus's job costing is genuinely impressive.

### ServiceM8 Job Costing

ServiceM8 has job costing functionality — time tracking, materials logging, and job cost summary — but it's less granular and less analytically rich than Fergus. If detailed margin analytics is your primary operational need, Fergus serves that purpose better.

For most trade businesses, ServiceM8's job costing is functional. But if you're the owner who wants to know your exact margin on every job, every week, broken down by technician and job type, Fergus delivers more.

## Scheduling and Dispatch

### ServiceM8 Dispatch Board

ServiceM8's dispatch board is a visual, real-time view of all jobs and all staff with GPS positions. For businesses managing emergency callouts, multi-technician scheduling, and dynamic rescheduling, this is a significant operational tool.

When an emergency job comes in, you see exactly which tech is closest, what they're doing, and when they'll be free. Reassignment takes seconds. The client gets an automated ETA notification without anyone making a call.

### Fergus Scheduling

Fergus has job scheduling functionality with a calendar view and team schedule management. It's functional and adequate for most small trade businesses. It doesn't have ServiceM8's real-time GPS dispatch board, which is a more meaningful gap for businesses managing higher job volumes or emergency callout work.

## FAQ

### Is Fergus available in Australia?

Yes. Fergus originated in New Zealand and operates in Australia. It's well established in the ANZ market with an Australian user base across electrical, plumbing, and general trade businesses.

### Does ServiceM8 have job costing?

Yes. ServiceM8 includes time tracking, materials logging, and job cost summary. It provides the fundamental job costing data that most small trade businesses need. If you require more granular margin analysis — profitability by technician, job type, or client segment — Fergus's reporting is more detailed.

### Can I switch from Fergus to ServiceM8?

Yes. ServiceM8's onboarding team can assist with migration. Client records and basic job history can be imported. Compliance form templates will need to be rebuilt in ServiceM8's form builder.

### Which platform has better customer support?

ServiceM8 is Australian-based with Australian support. Fergus has AU-based support as well. Both platforms have responsive support reputations. ServiceM8's larger Australian user base generally means more community resources (forum posts, help articles, YouTube walkthroughs) specific to Australian compliance workflows.
