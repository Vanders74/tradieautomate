Painting is a volume business. Most painting contractors are doing dozens of jobs a month — residential repaint, new construction, commercial maintenance, strata work. The trade itself is skilled but the business model runs on throughput, and anything that slows the throughput kills the margin.

The admin overhead in a painting business is higher than people expect: quoting across multiple sites, scheduling crews, ordering materials, tracking job progress across multiple simultaneous sites, invoicing clients, and chasing the slow payers.

Done manually, across a growing team, this can easily consume 15–20 hours a week of the owner's time. Time that should be going into quoting new work, managing client relationships, or simply not working on weekends.

## What Painters Actually Need From Job Management Software

Painting businesses have specific operational requirements that generic invoicing software doesn't address:

**Fast, professional on-site quoting.** The painter who can hand a quote to a homeowner before leaving the property wins the job at a significantly higher rate than the one who sends an email three days later. Mobile quoting is table stakes for competitive painting contractors.

**Multi-site crew scheduling.** Once you're running 2–3 crews simultaneously, manually coordinating who's at which site, when, and what they're doing becomes a daily juggling act. You need visibility and fast reassignment when things shift.

**Photo documentation.** Before-and-after photos are core to painting business documentation — for client sign-off, insurance purposes, and resolving disputes about scope. These photos need to be attached to specific jobs, not scattered across devices.

**Materials tracking.** Paint, primers, tape, and consumables are tracked per job to manage cost and ensure profitability. If you're not tracking materials job by job, you're flying blind on margin.

**Fast invoicing.** Painting jobs at the lower end of the ticket range ($1,000–$5,000) have thin enough margins that payment terms matter a lot. Getting invoices out same-day, with easy payment options, directly improves cash flow.

ServiceM8 addresses all of these with native features built for trade businesses.

## Quoting: Win More Jobs, Faster

The single biggest competitive advantage in the painting industry is response speed at the quoting stage. Studies consistently show that the first trade to submit a professional quote wins the job far more often than the cheapest quote that arrives three days later.

ServiceM8's mobile quoting lets your estimator or site supervisor build itemised quotes on-site:

- Materials with cost and markup (paint, primer, tape, drop sheets)

The quote is professional and branded. It can be emailed to the client before your team leaves the driveway. The client accepts via a digital link, and the quote converts to a job in one tap — no re-entry, all data carries forward.

## Photo Documentation: Before, During, After

Before-and-after photos serve multiple purposes in a painting business:

1. **Client sign-off** — visual evidence that the scope was completed as quoted
2. **Dispute resolution** — when a client claims something wasn't painted or that prep work wasn't done
3. **Surface condition documentation** — photographing existing damage before paint work starts protects against claims that the painter damaged the surface
4. **Insurance purposes** — commercial work often requires documented evidence of surface preparation
5. **Quality control** — supervisors can review photos from any job, from any location

In ServiceM8, photos taken through the mobile app are automatically attached to the specific job record with a timestamp and GPS location. Surface condition photos at job commencement, progress photos at each stage, and final completion photos are all captured and associated with the correct job.

No more "where's the before photo from the Mosman job?" Six months later, you open the job record and they're right there.

## Same-Day Invoicing

For painting jobs from $2,000 to $20,000+, getting the invoice out the same day the job is completed — rather than at the end of the week — materially improves cash flow. When you're doing 30 jobs a month, this difference can represent tens of thousands of dollars in working capital timing.

ServiceM8's automatic invoicing workflow:

5. Payment syncs to Xero or MYOB automatically

For residential clients who expected a same-day invoice, this is simply professional. For property managers and strata managers who manage a budget, same-day invoicing makes their job easier and builds your relationship.

Payment reminders for overdue invoices go out automatically. You've stopped chasing most of your debtors before you even notice they're late.

## Materials Tracking for Margin Control

Paint and consumables can represent 15–25% of job costs for interior residential work, and significantly more for specialty finishes or commercial work. If you're not tracking materials per job, you're estimating margin — not knowing it.

ServiceM8 lets technicians and crew leaders log materials against each job — product name, litres used, cost. This feeds into job costing so you can see actual versus estimated cost for each job.

Over time, this data becomes your estimating reference. You know that a standard 3-bedroom interior costs approximately X litres of paint, Y hours of labour, and produces Z margin at your quoted rates. Estimating becomes more accurate. Margin surprises become rare.

## FAQ

### Can ServiceM8 handle multi-day painting jobs?

Yes. ServiceM8 supports multi-day jobs with task breakdowns by day. Crew leaders update status each day, which gives the office visibility into progress without requiring check-in calls. Photos from each day are attached to the job record chronologically.

### Does ServiceM8 work for large commercial painting contracts?

ServiceM8 works well for commercial maintenance painting and smaller commercial contracts. For very large-scale commercial painting projects with complex project management, progress claim administration, and subcontractor coordination, a dedicated construction project management platform may provide more depth.

### Can I manage multiple crews from ServiceM8?

Yes. ServiceM8's dispatch board shows all crews and jobs simultaneously, with GPS tracking per crew leader. You can see who's at which site, their current status, and reassign or reschedule from the same screen.

### How does ServiceM8 handle quote variations?

When additional scope is identified on-site (e.g., a wall that needs plastering before painting), the crew leader can create a variation from within the job. The variation is presented to the client for approval — digitally, before any additional work starts — and approved variations add to the job total and final invoice automatically.
